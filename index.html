Introduction

Badminton is derived from the game "Poona" or "Poonah". It was developed in the mid-19th century as a variant of the earlier game of "battledore and shuttlecock". The term battledore refers to the rackets used to play this sport. The most common games can be played in singles or doubles, which is typically done in outdoor settings.

Facts

Ancient Sport

  • Shuttlecock games were played more than 2000 years ago in Greece, China, and India.
  • Evolved from the ancient game of battledore and shuttlecock.
  • Played in Medieval England by the late 16th century, where it became popular among children.

Source: BHF

Lifestyle

Popularity

Rules

These are basic rules in badminton as set by the Badminton World Federation (BWF):

  1. The match is best of three games, each to 21 points.
  2. Points are scored on every serve (rally scoring system).
  3. A serve must be hit underarm and below the serverâ€™s waist.
  4. The serve must travel diagonally across the court.
  5. If the shuttlecock lands outside the boundary lines, the rally ends.
  6. Players must not touch the net with their racket or body during play.
  7. Only one hit per side is allowed before the shuttle crosses the net.
  8. The shuttle must not be carried or held on the racket.

Note: Official tournament matches may have additional detailed rules and regulations.

Equipment

Racket

A lightweight frame used to hit the shuttlecock, typically made from carbon fiber or aluminum.

Shuttlecock

A conical projectile made of feathers or synthetic materials, used in gameplay.

Net

A mesh divider set at 1.55 meters in height, separating the two sides of the court.

Badminton Shoes

Non-marking shoes that provide grip and cushioning for quick movements on court.
